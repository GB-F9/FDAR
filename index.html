What is FDAR Charting?
FDAR is a systematic method of nursing documentation ensuring every note is complete and legally sound. Fill in the Clinical Information fields and tap Auto-fill to generate a ready-to-edit draft.
F
FOCUS
Primary subject of the entry
"Acute pain related to post-operative wound, pain scale 8/10, patient appears restless and diaphoretic."
Can be a nursing diagnosis, symptom, or clinical concern
Be specific — not just "pain" but "acute pain r/t ___"
Can also be a significant event (fall, code blue)
D
DATA
Subjective and objective evidence
"S: Patient verbalized 'The pain is unbearable, I cannot sleep.'
O: BP 140/90, HR 102, Temp 37.8°C. Wound site erythematous."
S — patient's own words in quotation marks
O — vital signs, physical findings, lab values
Report facts only — no interpretation
A
ACTION
All interventions performed
"Administered Ketorolac 30mg IV as ordered. Repositioned patient. Dr. Reyes notified — ordered dressing change."
List ALL actions — meds, positioning, monitoring, education
Include time of medication administration
Document physician notification and orders received
R
RESPONSE
Patient's response to interventions
"Patient reported pain decreased to 4/10 after 30 min. BP normalized to 120/80. Verbalized understanding of wound care."
Compare to initial data (e.g., pain 8/10 → 4/10)
Include patient's verbal response
If no improvement — note it and document follow-up
